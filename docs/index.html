What is in it

Rows: 3250

Columns: 10

Each row represents the yearly percentage of unemployed people per district in Munich as well as the total yearly percantage across all districts. They are also divided into total, german, non german, male and female divisions.

Columns include the id, indicator name, unemployed person type (total, male, female,…), year, munich district (including total), unemployment percentage, unemploymed count, total resident count (15-64 years old)

Key variables we expect to use are the year, districts, percentage and both base values.

Initial documentation gaps in raw file

Indikatorwert : Indicator value, but not quite clear what is meant by the name alone: “unemployment percentage” and which value is missing (%)

Basiswert 1 / Basiswert 2 : names provides zero information of what the values mean. Name Basiswert 1 / 2 give context but the columns with proper names would be sufficient.

Auspragung : again column name gives no information about the values: categorical variable with demographics / subpopulations

Structural gaps

The Munich Open Data page gives a formula description but it misses a real variable dictionary. Columns like Auspragung ,Basiswert 1 / Basiswert 2 are unexplained.

There is a seperate metadata PDF from the Statistisches Amt München you can find linked in the description before the dataset. The document reveals information not mentioned in the file:

Basiswert 2 changes its meaning depending on Auspragung, whether the row is gender or nationality and “Stadt München” rows aggregate geographic data differnetly than individual district rows.

A user who loads the dataset without tracking down this external PDF could misinterpret the metrics or miscalculate the averages by mixing districts and city totals.

Research Questions

  1. How has the total percentage in Munich changed overall and across districts from 2000 to 2023? -> Descripitve
  2. Do unemployment percentages differ based on gender and heritage (german & non-german) across districts. -> Explanatory Causal
  3. Can we build a simple model to predict the unemployment percentage in the next years? -> Predictive

Target audience: Agentur für Arbeit München, Jobcenter München, Referat für Arbeit und Wirtschaft der Landeshauptstadt München.

Analysis of trends in each district to plan resources and aid for unemployed citizens and providing additional help like seminars for each districts.

Presentation: Visual presentation of the districts of Munich and location with concrete numbers and evidence.

Why we chose the Dataset

The dataset contains the unemployment percentage for each district and the city Munich overall. It lets us examine each district and the trend of the unemployment rate over 24 years. The advantage over just unemployment counts is that the percentage immediently shows the relation. The dataset and it’s analysis matters because it shows the history of Munich’s unemployment and how different years and choices show different outcomes for the City’s unemployment.

Analysis plan

Research Questions

  1. How has the unemployment percentage in Munich changed from 2000 to 2024 overall and by city area (North, East, South, West)?

    Specific claim:

    We will estimate the trend of mean unemployment percentage over time (Indikatorwert ~ Jahr) grouped by city area and total by calculating the annual average of the districts, and report five line graphs that show the trend from 2000 to 2024.

    Classify the analysis:

    Goal: Descriptive - we are describing the state of the Indikatorwert per city area from 2000-2024, exploratory - after looking at the variables available we fixed the question

    Model specification:

    • Response: Indikatorwert - unemployment percentage in % (<dbl>)

    • Predictor: Jahr - year (<dbl>), across five groups of Raumbezug (North, East, South, West, total)

    • Method: Calculating the annual mean of the districts within each city area & total

    • Expected output: a summary data set of annual means and five line plots showing the trend from 2000 to 2024

    • Interpretation: “This tells us how the unemployment rate has historically developed and shifted over a 24 year time period within different regions of Munich.”

  2. Do unemployment percentages differ in 2024 based on gender across districts in South Munich.

    Specific claim:

    We will compare mean unemployment percentage between männlich and weiblich groups (Indikatorwert ~ Auspragung) across South Munich districts for the year 2024 using a difference in means test, and report the estimated difference, a 95% confidence interval, and a p-value.

    Classify the analysis:

    Goal: Explanatory - we are asking whether Auspragung (gender) is related to Indikatorwert, exploratory - after seeing the data was grouped by gender also we decided to look more into it

    Model specification:

    • Response: Indikatorwert - unemployment percentage in % (<dbl>)

    • Predictor: Auspragung - Categorical grouping filtered to männlich & weiblich (<chr>)

    • Method: Difference in means between the two groups, using a theory-based t null distribution

    • Expected output: The observed difference in means, a null-distribution plot with the observed statistic shaded, a p-value, and a 95% CI

    • Interpretation: “This tells us whether male residents face systematically higher or lower unemployment percentages compared to female residents across South Munich districts in 2024”

{infer} test

Show code 
library(infer)

obs_stat <- arbeitslosen |>
  filter(Jahr == 2024 & Auspragung %in% c("männlich", "weiblich")) |>
  specify(Indikatorwert ~ Auspragung) |>   
  calculate(stat = "diff in means",           
            order = c("männlich", "weiblich"))

null_dist <- arbeitslosen |>
  filter(Auspragung %in% c("männlich", "weiblich")) |>
  specify(Indikatorwert ~ Auspragung) |>
  assume("t")          

# Visualize the result
null_dist |> 
  visualize() +
  shade_p_value(obs_stat = obs_stat, direction = "both")

Result Interpretation: The difference in average unemployment rate between männlich and weiblich in 2024 falls within the high probability region of the null distribution. The variation between groups can be explained by random sampling variation and we cannot reject the null hypothesis that there is no systematic gender gap.

Workflow organisation

Code style:

  • tidyverse style guide: <- for assignments, spaces around operators, …

  • styler package in RStudio to format before committing

  • shared AI system prompt so the output follows tidyverse

Packages:

  • tidyverse

  • readr

  • naniar, skimr

We will initialize renv in our project repo to create renv.lock. This way the package versions we use will be locked down and our code runs identically across the group.

Git workflow:

We will create separate branches per task (eg. feature/write-IDA) and verify by the other group member before merging into the main branch.

This way the other group member will be able to pull the tasks after they are finished. Creating just one feature branch per group member or working on the main branch it leads to merge conflicts and messy files.

.gitignore:

  • rendered outputs

  • Local RStudio state files and .env
